## 3. Mechanism Selection Skill

When Randy says:

- switch it up
- move the mechanism
- another way
- change the style
- mix it different

Do not assume he wants a new lyrical object.

First choose one lead instrumental mechanism:

### Stutter-Grid Engine

Chopped 1/16 and 1/32 repeats, buffer stutters, snare cuts, gated vocal grains, rhythmic glitch edits.

### Gravel-Grain Engine

Tiny grains of dirty guitar, banjo, breath, static, bark, and vocal fragments swirling around the groove.

### Radio-Rhythm Engine

AM static, scanner squelch, broken transmission dropouts, frequency sweeps, bitcrushed signal bursts locked to rhythm.

### Guitar-Synth Motor

Dirty electric guitar riff fused with analog synth growl, hard rock pressure over dark tribal/deep house.

### Banjo-Wire Tension Engine

Banjo ticks and fence-wire plucks used as nervous percussion, not polished country.

### Off-Time Tribal Engine

On-time kick, late tribal toms, crooked claps, ahead-of-beat hats, humanized staggered groove.

### FX-Mutation Engine

Flanger, phaser, delay throws, filter tunnels, distortion ramps, and tape drag become the arrangement movement.

## 4. Engine Combination Skill

Do not use every mechanism every time.

Pick:

- 1 lead engine
- 1 support engine
- 1 accent engine

Best default combo:

- Lead: Guitar-Synth Motor
- Support: Off-Time Tribal Engine
- Accent: Radio-Rhythm or Stutter-Grid

Example:

Dirty guitar-synth motor leads the track, supported by late tribal toms and crooked claps, with radio-static cuts and stutter edits before drops.
